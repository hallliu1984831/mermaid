## 引言：三个故事

最近几天，三个看似独立的故事在我脑海中不断交织，最终拼成了一幅令人略感焦虑的完整图景。

第一个故事发生在2021年。当时疫情肆虐，大多数人还在为生计发愁，OpenAI的CEO Sam Altman却发布了一篇名为《万物摩尔定律》的文章，大胆预言AI将带来巨大的社会变化。当时，很多人觉得这不过是硅谷精英的"画大饼"——毕竟GPT-3刚刚发布，普通人根本接触不到。

Tips: Citadel Securities是一家总部位于迈阿密的全球领先做市商和流动性提供商，为零售和机构投资者提供股票、固定收益、期权和期货交易服务。它是全球最大的金融公司之一，通常处理约占美国股票交易总量25%的交易，并提供高速、技术驱动的交易执行。

Block裁员
第三个故事就发生在同一个月。2026年2月，Twitter联合创始人Jack Dorsey突然宣布，他的金融科技公司Block将裁员40%——整整4000人。更震撼的是，他明确表示这是因为"AI和自动化集成"的需要。Block的股价随即上涨，市场用最直接的方式表达了对这种"效率提升"的认可。
Tips: Block, Inc.（前身为Square, Inc.）是一家美国大型金融服务和科技公司。该公司致力于通过一系列业务赋能经济，这些业务包括Square（商户POS系统）、Cash App（消费金融）、TIDAL（音乐）和Spiral（比特币）。

当这三个故事放在一起时，让人震惊的事实浮现出来：科幻预言正在以超出所有人想象的速度变成来到你我的身边；AI的进化速度远超普通人的预期（按月计）；延续一年之久的裁员潮还在继续。

## 第三章：软件行业的全面冲击

在这三个故事交织的背景下，一个残酷的现实正在浮现：软件行业正在经历一场前所未有的全面冲击。从前端到后端，从开发到运维，从技术到产品，没有任何一个岗位能够独善其身。

这不是危言耸听，而是正在发生的现实。当我们仔细观察各个技术岗位的变化时，会发现一个令人不安的趋势：AI的渗透速度远超所有人的预期。

### 软件行业面临的根本性转变

从"如何做"到"做什么"的价值重心转移

软件行业正在经历一个根本性的价值重心转移。过去，我们的价值主要体现在"如何实现"——如何写出高效的代码，如何设计优雅的架构，如何优化系统性能。但现在，AI正在快速掌握这些"如何做"的技能。

真正的价值开始向"做什么"转移：
- 理解业务需求的本质
- 识别真正需要解决的问题
- 做出正确的技术选择和权衡
- 协调人与AI的协作关系

技能半衰期的急剧缩短

传统上，一个技术人掌握的核心技能可能有5-10年的有效期。但在AI时代，这个周期正在急剧缩短。具体的编程技巧、框架和工具使用和架构设计模式等码农引以为傲的护城河正在被AI高效替代，只有业务理解和问题解决思维相对稳定。

从以码农为主到协作模式的转变

软件开发正在从"码农主导"转向"人机协作"模式：
- 过去：一个优秀的程序员团队可以独立完成复杂项目
- 现在：需要学会如何与AI协作，发挥各自优势
- 未来：可能需要管理多个AI助手，成为"AI团队"的协调者

### AI软件开发的三个时代

2026年2月26日，Cursor公司发布了一篇题为《AI软件开发的第三个时代》的博客文章，为我们提供了AI辅助软件开发演进的清晰时间线：

第一时代：Tab自动补全时代（2022-2024）
- 代码仍然是一个字符一个字符地写
- Tab自动补全改变了这一切，开启了AI辅助编程的第一个时代
- 持续了近两年，主要解决低熵、重复性工作的自动化

第二时代：同步Agent时代（2024-2025）
- 开发者转向通过同步提示-响应循环指导AI Agent
- Agent能够处理更多上下文，使用更多工具，执行更长的操作序列
- 2025年3月，Cursor的Tab用户是Agent用户的2.5倍
- 到2026年2月，这个比例完全翻转：Agent用户是Tab用户的2倍
- Agent使用量在一年内增长了15倍以上

第三时代：云端自主Agent时代（2026-现在）
- Agent能够独立处理更大的任务，时间跨度更长，人类指导更少
- 每个Agent在自己的虚拟机上运行，开发者可以交付任务后转向其他工作
- Agent工作数小时，迭代和测试直到对输出有信心，返回可快速审查的内容
- 关键数据：Cursor内部35%的PR现在由在云端VM中自主运行的Agent创建
TIPS: PR是pull request，代码合并的请求。

现实中的"温水煮青蛙"时间线

```
2020-2021：AI还是实验室玩具，技术人觉得"与我无关"
2022：GitHub Copilot出现，"还好只是代码补全"
2023：ChatGPT爆火，"复杂业务逻辑还是需要人"
2024：GPT-4/Claude能做架构设计，同步Agent开始普及
2025：Agent使用量暴增15倍，"至少创意和管理还安全"
2026：35%的PR由AI自主创建，软件行业裁员此起彼伏，并且延伸到其他行业
2027-2028：？？？
```

最震撼的现实：Cursor的开发者现在的工作特征是：
1. Agent写几乎100%的代码
2. 他们花时间分解问题、审查产出物、给出反馈
3. 他们同时启动多个Agent，而不是手把手指导一个Agent完成任务

这意味着软件开发的本质已经从"写代码"转变为"管理AI工厂"：开发者成为了AI Agent团队的协调者和审查者。

### 技术人的独特"诅咒"

作为软件行业的从业者，我们面临着一个残酷的悖论：
- 我们最了解AI的能力，所以最清楚被替代的可能性
- 我们创造了替代自己的工具，却无法阻止这个过程
- 我们的工作本质就是自动化，现在轮到我们被自动化了
- 我们习惯了快速学习新技术，但这次学习速度跟不上淘汰速度

## 第四章：更深层的思考

人类历史上第一次面临"智能过剩"：

# The AI Era's "Three-Body Crisis": When "Your Destruction Has Nothing to Do with You" Slowly Approaches

## Introduction: Three Stories

Over the past few days, three seemingly independent stories have been weaving together in my mind, ultimately forming a complete picture that leaves me somewhat anxious.

Moore's Law for Everything
The first story happened in 2021. While the pandemic was raging and most people were worried about making ends meet, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman published an article called "Moore's Law for Everything," boldly predicting that AI would bring massive social changes. At the time, many people thought it was just another Silicon Valley elite "pipe dream"—after all, GPT-3 had just been released and ordinary people couldn't even access it.

The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis
The second story comes from a thought experiment article published in February 2026 called "The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis." The author, writing from a 2028 perspective, depicted a hypothetical scenario where AI triggers a global economic crisis: white-collar jobs disappear en masse, financial markets collapse, and society falls into an "intelligence replacement spiral."
The article's impact far exceeded expectations—immediately after publication, it triggered violent stock market volatility, with the Dow Jones index plunging, becoming the focus of everyone's attention. Citadel Securities even published a special report refuting this "virally spreading doomsday paper."
Tips: Citadel Securities is a Miami-based global leading market maker and liquidity provider, offering equity, fixed income, options, and futures trading services to retail and institutional investors. It's one of the world's largest financial companies, typically handling about 25% of total U.S. equity trading volume and providing high-speed, technology-driven trade execution.

Block Layoffs
The third story happened in the same month. In February 2026, Twitter co-founder Jack Dorsey suddenly announced that his fintech company Block would lay off 40%—a full 4,000 people. Even more shocking, he explicitly stated this was due to the need for "AI and automation integration." Block's stock price immediately rose, with the market expressing its approval of this "efficiency improvement" in the most direct way.
Tips: Block, Inc. (formerly Square, Inc.) is a major American financial services and technology company. The company is committed to empowering the economy through a series of businesses including Square (merchant POS systems), Cash App (consumer finance), TIDAL (music), and Spiral (bitcoin).

When these three stories are put together, a shocking fact emerges: science fiction prophecies are coming to our doorstep at a speed beyond everyone's imagination; AI's evolution speed far exceeds ordinary people's expectations (measured in months); the layoff wave that has lasted for a year continues.

In the science fiction novel "The Three-Body Problem," "Your destruction has nothing to do with you" reveals the cruelty of dimensional reduction attacks—higher civilizations destroy lower civilizations not out of malice, but out of efficiency. In the AI era, we see the same logic:

Block's layoffs aren't malicious, they're mathematical:
- $200/month AI agent vs $180,000/year product manager
- This isn't an HR decision, it's algorithmic optimization
- Laid-off employees aren't even in the decision-makers' consideration

The feedback loop described in "The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" is already taking shape:
```
AI gets better and cheaper → Companies lay off staff to save costs → Use saved money to buy more AI → AI continues to improve → Loop accelerates
```

Block's stock price rose after the layoffs, with the market rewarding this "efficiency improvement." This signal will encourage more companies to follow suit, as its boss Jack Dorsey predicted: "Within the next year, I believe most companies will reach the same conclusion."

## Chapter 3: Comprehensive Impact on the Software Industry

Against the backdrop of these three intertwining stories, a harsh reality is emerging: the software industry is experiencing an unprecedented comprehensive impact. From frontend to backend, from development to operations, from technical to product roles, no position can remain unaffected.

This isn't fear-mongering, but reality unfolding. When we carefully observe changes across various technical positions, we discover an unsettling trend: AI's penetration speed far exceeds everyone's expectations.

### Fundamental Transformations Facing the Software Industry

Shift in Value Focus from "How to Do" to "What to Do"

The software industry is experiencing a fundamental shift in value focus. In the past, our value was mainly reflected in "how to implement"—how to write efficient code, how to design elegant architecture, how to optimize system performance. But now, AI is rapidly mastering these "how to do" skills.

Real value is beginning to shift toward "what to do":
- Understanding the essence of business requirements
- Identifying problems that truly need solving
- Making correct technical choices and trade-offs
- Coordinating human-AI collaboration relationships

Dramatic Shortening of Skill Half-Life

Traditionally, a tech professional's core skills might have a 5-10 year validity period. But in the AI era, this cycle is dramatically shortening. Specific programming techniques, framework and tool usage, and architectural design patterns—these moats that programmers take pride in—are being efficiently replaced by AI, with only business understanding and problem-solving thinking remaining relatively stable.

Shift from Programmer-Dominated to Collaborative Models

Software development is shifting from "programmer-dominated" to "human-machine collaboration" models:
- Past: An excellent programming team could independently complete complex projects
- Present: Need to learn how to collaborate with AI, leveraging respective strengths
- Future: May need to manage multiple AI assistants, becoming "AI team" coordinators

### Three Eras of AI Software Development

On February 26, 2026, Cursor published a blog post titled "The Third Era of AI Software Development," providing us with a clear timeline of AI-assisted software development evolution:

First Era: Tab Autocomplete Era (2022-2024)
- Code was still written character by character
- Tab autocomplete changed everything, opening the first era of AI-assisted programming
- Lasted nearly two years, mainly solving automation of low-entropy, repetitive work

Second Era: Synchronous Agent Era (2024-2025)
- Developers shifted to directing AI agents through synchronous prompt-response loops
- Agents could handle more context, use more tools, execute longer operation sequences
- In March 2025, Cursor had 2.5x as many Tab users as Agent users
- By February 2026, this ratio completely flipped: 2x as many Agent users as Tab users
- Agent usage grew over 15x in one year

Third Era: Cloud Autonomous Agent Era (2026-Present)
- Agents can independently handle larger tasks over longer timeframes with less human direction
- Each agent runs on its own virtual machine, allowing developers to hand off tasks and move to other work
- Agents work for hours, iterating and testing until confident in output, returning quickly reviewable content
- Key data: 35% of PRs at Cursor are now created by agents operating autonomously in cloud VMs
TIPS: PR stands for pull request, a code merge request.

Real-World "Boiling Frog" Timeline

```
2020-2021: AI still a lab toy, tech people think "doesn't concern me"
2022: GitHub Copilot appears, "at least it's just code completion"
2023: ChatGPT explodes, "complex business logic still needs humans"
2024: GPT-4/Claude can do architectural design, synchronous agents start spreading
2025: Agent usage surges 15x, "at least creativity and management are still safe"
2026: 35% of PRs created by AI autonomously, software industry layoffs one after another, extending to other industries
2027-2028: ???
```

Most Shocking Reality: Cursor developers now work with these characteristics:
1. Agents write almost 100% of the code
2. They spend time breaking down problems, reviewing outputs, giving feedback
3. They spin up multiple agents simultaneously instead of hand-holding one agent to completion

This means software development's essence has shifted from "writing code" to "managing AI factories": developers have become coordinators and reviewers of AI agent teams.
